# Medical Cannabis Inventory Program

## Safety Rules

This is a personal tracking and harm-reduction program, not medical advice. Confirm dosing with your certifying clinician, especially if you take medications for blood pressure, mood, sleep, pain, diabetes, or heart issues.

Core rules:

1. Do not drive, work, exercise heavily, or make important decisions after THC use.
2. Do not mix cannabis with alcohol.
3. Dose by **THC mg**, not just grams.
4. Use the smallest dose that gives the needed medical effect.
5. Do not redose too quickly.
6. Track the effect every time.
7. Cannabis should support sleep, pain control, appetite control, and recovery. If it hurts productivity, diet, or workouts, it is failing the mission.

Health Canada’s lower-risk guidance says to “start low and go slow,” begin with a low amount of THC, and wait to feel effects before taking more. Ottawa Public Health also classifies flower above 20% THC as “very strong” and suggests starting with only 1–2 puffs when smoking or vaping. Edibles/capsules can take much longer to fully hit, and CDC material notes ingested THC has a slower onset and longer duration than smoked cannabis.  
Sources: Health Canada, Ottawa Public Health, CDC.

# Important Dose Notes

## Flower THC Estimate

Use this formula:

```text
grams used × 1000 × THC percentage = THC in the flower
````

Examples:

| Flower THC % |     0.025g |      0.05g |    0.10g |
| -----------: | ---------: | ---------: | -------: |
|      20% THC |    5mg THC |   10mg THC | 20mg THC |
|      25% THC | 6.25mg THC | 12.5mg THC | 25mg THC |
|      30% THC |  7.5mg THC |   15mg THC | 30mg THC |
|      33% THC | 8.25mg THC | 16.5mg THC | 33mg THC |

Smoking/vaping does not deliver all of that THC into your bloodstream, but the product is still potent. Do not treat 0.10g as meaningless.

## RSO Capsule Warning

Each RSO capsule is **50mg THC**, based on the package description.

That is a large oral dose.

For many people:

| THC Oral Dose | Practical Meaning             |
| ------------: | ----------------------------- |
|         2.5mg | Beginner / cautious test      |
|           5mg | Light dose                    |
|          10mg | Standard moderate edible dose |
|          20mg | Strong                        |
|          50mg | Very strong                   |

Do not take a 50mg capsule on a workday, before responsibilities, before driving, or before an early morning. If 50mg is too strong, ask your dispensary/pharmacist whether the product can be safely divided. Do not assume every capsule can be split evenly.

## Goal

Use cannabis only in a way that supports:

* focus
* pain control
* anxiety control
* sleep
* workout recovery
* appetite control
* fat loss
* consistent routines

Cannabis should not become the reason you eat more, move less, or waste your best mental hours.

# Daily Structure

## Morning: No Cannabis Block

**Time:** Wake-up through first major work block

**Rule:** No THC.

Use this block for:

* planning
* important work
* meetings
* workouts
* decision-making
* calorie planning
* protein-first meals

Cannabis is not allowed here unless your clinician specifically directed daytime use for a medical reason.

---

## Midday / Early Afternoon: Functional Test Block

**Use only if symptoms are interfering with productivity.**

Best candidates:

1. Jungle Pie
2. Cheddar Cheeze, only after testing
3. Cereal Milk, only one tiny puff if absolutely needed

Starting protocol:

```text
Take 1 small puff OR 0.025g–0.05g flower.
Wait 15 minutes.
Do not redose unless the symptom is still present.
Stop at 0.05g during testing.
```

Use for:

* mild anxiety
* pain that is distracting
* mood lift
* creative work
* low-stakes admin work

Avoid for:

* coding/debugging that needs precision
* financial decisions
* difficult conversations
* management escalations
* driving
* workouts

---

## Late Afternoon / Transition Block

**Time:** After your most important work is done.

Best candidates:

1. Jungle Pie
2. Double Baked Cake, low dose
3. Cereal Milk, 1 puff only

Use this block to transition out of work without wrecking the evening.

Good activities:

* walk
* stretching
* meal prep
* light chores
* journaling
* low-stakes creative work

Bad activities:

* ordering food
* grocery shopping while high
* snacking without tracking
* lying on the couch for 3 hours

---

## Evening / Night Recovery Block

**Time:** After dinner, after responsibilities, no driving.

Best candidates:

1. Sunday Gravy
2. Double Baked Cake
3. Cereal Milk infused pre-roll
4. RSO capsule only when you have no early obligation the next day

Starting protocol for flower:

```text
0.05g max to start.
Wait 15 minutes.
Only increase if needed.
Avoid going above 0.10g until you know the strain.
```

Starting protocol for infused pre-roll:

```text
1 small puff.
Put it out.
Wait 15–20 minutes.
Do not smoke it like a normal joint.
```

Starting protocol for RSO:

```text
Avoid routine use.
Use only on dedicated night-recovery days.
Do not combine with smoked flower until you know its effect.
Expect long duration.
Track next-day grogginess.
```

# Food and Weight-Loss Guardrails

Cannabis can quietly sabotage fat loss through munchies. This program only works if cannabis is paired with food rules.

## Before Cannabis

Complete this checklist:

```text
[ ] Protein target planned
[ ] Dinner planned or already eaten
[ ] Snacks pre-portioned
[ ] Water nearby
[ ] No food delivery apps opened
[ ] No grocery shopping after use
```

## Approved High-Satiety Snacks

Use these if hungry after cannabis:

* Greek yogurt
* protein shake
* cottage cheese
* fruit
* carrots / cucumbers
* lean turkey/chicken
* air-popped popcorn, portioned
* sparkling water
* herbal tea

## Avoid After Cannabis

* chips
* cookies
* cereal
* ice cream
* pizza
* DoorDash/Uber Eats
* “I’ll just have a little” foods

That sentence is where the diet dies.

# Decision Rules

## Keep Product If

```text
Medical benefit is 7/10 or higher
AND productivity loss is 3/10 or lower
AND munchies are 4/10 or lower
AND next-day grogginess is 3/10 or lower
```

## Limit Product If

```text
It helps symptoms but causes munchies, laziness, anxiety, or next-day fog.
Use only at night or on low-responsibility days.
```

## Avoid Product If

```text
It causes anxiety, paranoia, binge eating, missed workouts, poor sleep, or next-day brain fog.
```
